BSAD 234, Business Law

This page outlines the class in a bit more detail, and will have links to handouts for each lesson posted as we proceed.

Each section of the course will have a short, open-everything-except-living-people exam following it. Think of these as mini midterms.

Lesson 1: What law is, where it comes from, how it works

In this section we'll cover "big picture" topics (the idea of law, sources of law, levels of authority), as well as particulars (how to read court cases, how citations work, etc).

The first three chapters will give us the broad basics of the legal system.

  • Chapter 1: An introduction to the idea of law, how to read cases, and so on.
  • Chapter 2: Discusses how disputes are resolved (e.g., courts versus arbitration)
  • Chapter 3: The basics of civil procedure (what happens when you choose to resolve a dispute in court)

We'll also spend a day outside the book, and I'll give you a history of the common law.

Lesson 2: Law stemming from the Constitution

This section will examine the law flowing from the Constitution: (1) constitutional law itself, particularly criminal procedure, (2) statutory interpretation through antitrust law, and (3) administrative law, particularly through environmental and employment law. The reading on the Constitution and statutory law is:

  • Chapter 4: The Constitution
  • Chapter 10: Criminal law
  • Chapter 46: Antitrust law
